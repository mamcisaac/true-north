How to play

Lay your phone flat and don't move it. The game secretly reads your compass — but never shows you north.

A real place is named. Drag the needle to point in the real-world direction you think it lies and lock it in. Then set how far away it is — up to 20,000 km, the far side of the world — and throw.

A globe appears, oriented the way your phone is actually facing. Your dart flies your chosen direction and distance along the curve of the Earth. The daily ranks you by your total distance from the target — how many km your darts landed from the mark across five places — so lower is better. Free play keeps a classic 0–5000 points score instead.

Your dart flies in a straight line — a great circle — but because the Earth is round, that straight path looks curved on a flat map. Fly far enough in any direction and you reach the far side of the world, 20,000 km away.

On a computer (no compass), the top of the screen counts as north.
